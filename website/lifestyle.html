How to PROPERLY compost

by By: Tahj Anderson

Posted on January 26, 2019 at 1:31 AM

A lot of people do not compost in their home at all. Millions and millions of unused components for compost go to waste in our landfills, adding a unnecessary amount of trash.

Composting is turning food scraps and trash into usuable soil. Composting has been proven to reduce methane and other green house gases in out air. It also leaves you with nutrient rich soil to use or donate.

Now that you know the importance it is time to learn what to compost and how to compost it. You can compost often "random" things in your house. Things like egg shells, cut flowers, coffee grounds, tea, teabags, and toothpicks!

The options are vast! Here is a full list to more items.
list of compostable items

Now there are two ways to compost. There is cold composting and hot composting. Cold composting is collecting the items listed above and letting them sit in a bin and decompose for a year. Hot composting requires four ingrediants. It uses nitrogen, carbon, air, and water. All of these feed the bacteria and speed up the process.

Now that you know how to compost, it is time to use your new skill. If you commit this to your life style you can develop habits that change the world.
